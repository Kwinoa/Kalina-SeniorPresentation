FOODS

I took Foods in my freshman and sophomore year for elective credits and just because it sounded fun. Some subjects that were taught in this class include reading nutrition labels, portioning food, roles of different ingredients in a recipe, substituting ingredients, converting units, sanitary kitchen practices, important cooking terms, knife skills, and more. This class was useful because I learned some life skills and am more aware of healthy sanitary and dietary habits.

INTERNSHIP

Internship was a required class for juniors. The main activities and projects in this class were creating a resume and cover letter, practicing interview skills, building a portfolio, and obviously completing the required internship hours. My internship was at an accounting firm, which started in the summer and lasted 2 weeks. This class was useful because I can use the materials in the future and because the internship was a valuable experience for me to explore career paths.

AP PSYCHOLOGY

I took AP psych for the college credit. Some things I learned were the different approaches to psychology, the important figures in psychology, nature vs. nurture, sensation and perception, developmental stages, experimentation ethics, the brain and body, stress and coping, bias, etc. This class was useful because it gave me a college credit and helped me better understand human behavior.

AP US HISTORY

US History and the APUSH test was one of the most difficult classes for me because there was so much content to absorb and because I wasn't very fond of DBQ's or essays. Some topics I vaguely remember learning about were European colonization, Native American tribes, Manifest Destiny, Immigration, Industrialization, Reconstruction, and The Great Awakening. This class was useful because I gained a deeper understanding of the United States' history and how it contributed to the complex society we live in today.

AP ENVIRONMENTAL SCIENCE

The last AP class I took was environmental science this year. The class was fun because of the labs and activities. Among the many units we learned this year were biodiversity, populations, energy and resource consumption, cycles, and pollution. But the biggest takeaway from this class was sustainability and finding solutions to environmental problems.

UOFU RACE AND GENDER

I took this class for a humanities college credit. I struggled a lot in this class for some reason, maybe because it was a more conceptual class and there was a lot of writing. But, some topics that was discussed in-class include marginality, feminism, oppressed vs oppressor, the model minority myth, intersectionality, colorism, and health inequalities. This was an important class because I think there are lots of relevant and important topics that was discussed in this class, and hearing other people's views and experiences helped me understand multiple perspectives.

UOFU WRITING

UofU Writing was another class that I think I had a hard time in, but I have always struggled with writing, so it was a given that this class would be a challenge. Moving on from the negatives, it was nice to be able to choose the topics of our paper and be flexible with the organization of it. The main assignments in this class were the collaborative writing assignment, personal research essay, monster essay, rhetorical analysis, lit review, argument paper, and also the presentations. This class was still beneficial to me because we explored lots of genres of writing and because it prepares me for college writing.
