Final Project

As the title states, this assignment was the final project for Painting II (and my favorite). The prompt was to paint anything you'd like, spend at least 2 hours, and use acrylic paint. I used a reference to paint this and took a good bit over 2 hours because I got too absorbed in the process, and also because mixing the right colors was a challenge.

White Rabbit

I painted this rabbit for a value painting. The assignment was to paint a black and white portrait in watercolor. I don't remember why I chose a rabbit, but I liked this painting because the black and white makes it so that you can focus on the details more, but also because painting in black and white is easier than color.

Cherry Blossoms

I personally don't like this work, but I included it because others said they liked it and it was a good opportunity to critique it. Like the previous, the assignment for this was to use watercolor to paint a landscape (also using a reference). Starting with the positives, I think I got the proportions and placement down pretty well. On the other hand, it looks unnatural and the colors are off.

Chairs & Table

If I remember correctly, this was part of some Sketchup 3D design practice where we had to make four chairs and a table, each with unique textures and features. I included it because it was different from the paintings that I did in my art classes. They may look good on the outside and from this angle, but there are actually many imperfections in their design if you look underneath.

Water Pollution

This was another final project for my world history class. If I remember correctly, my idea for the project was to connect the industrial revolution to water pollution. We were given free reign to do anything, and I chose to crochet because it didn't require me to look at a screen, so I could just mindlessly move my hands.

Two-Pager

I don't remember exactly what the prompt of this assignment was, but I'm guessing that we had to create a two-page infographic on some topic. My topic was probably to show how advancements in weaponry have contributed to deadlier wars. There's not much to say other than that my handwriting doesn't look that great.
